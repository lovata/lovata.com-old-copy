About LOVATA and Web Accessibility

Since the introduction of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) in 1990, businesses have made many changes in the way they accommodate the physically or visually impaired people and hard of hearing individuals. Stores and other physical locations put forth various mechanisms to ensure disabled people have the same opportunities to acquire products and services as regular people. It is not just a legal obligation, but also a moral duty for all of us to provide these people with a friendly and convenient environment so they could lead their lives without being bound by the restraints their disability imposes on them.

With the advent and rapid proliferation of the internet, most impaired people now prefer to obtain services and order goods online. Of course, they also would like to have the same experience while browsing the web for educational, entertainment and other purposes as any other individual. Thanks to the ADA legislation and the WCAG 2.0 web accessibility guidelines set forth by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), businesses now have a responsibility and an opportunity to make their web-based services fully available to individuals with disabilities.

LOVATA puts ADA website compliance at the forefront of every design and development. Thanks to our rich experience working with clients across the globe, we have become one of the industry’s best accessibility experts. Our achievements have made us a Global Leader in Software and Web Development. With the help of this scholarship program, LOVATA Company is raising awareness of web accessibility among young students while at the same time helping gifted individuals to achieve their goals in becoming highly skilled professionals in their field.
